            - heading "Spreads" [level=2] [ref=e63]
            - paragraph [ref=e64]: The spread is the difference between the buy and sell price of an asset. It is the primary way Capital·com generates revenue. The size varies depending on the asset and market conditions.
            - paragraph [ref=e65]: In highly liquid markets such as EUR/USD or gold, spreads are typically lower than in less liquid markets. Less liquid markets may carry higher costs and greater volatility.
            - paragraph [ref=e66]: The spread on the deal ticket before opening a position shows the exact cost at that moment.
            - generic [ref=e67]:
              - heading "CFD spread example" [level=3] [ref=e68]

                - listitem [ref=e71]: • The spread is 1 point.
                - listitem [ref=e72]: • Half the spread is paid on opening and half on closing. The total spread cost is A$1 x 1 point = A$1.
            - heading "Guaranteed stop-loss orders" [level=2] [ref=e73]
            - paragraph [ref=e74]: A standard stop-loss order closes a position at a specified level. It is not guaranteed to execute at exactly that price — during a market gap, execution may occur at the next available price. Slippage can occur in volatile or low-liquidity conditions.
            - paragraph [ref=e75]: A guaranteed stop-loss order (GSL) closes a position at exactly the specified price, regardless of slippage or market gaps. A fee — the GSL premium — applies if the order is triggered.
            - paragraph [ref=e76]
            - heading "Overnight funding" [level=3] [ref=e77]
            - paragraph [ref=e78]: Overnight funding is a daily charge applied to leveraged positions held past the daily rollover time. It reflects the cost of maintaining exposure outside standard market hours. In some cases — typically for short positions — a credit is applied rather than a charge.
